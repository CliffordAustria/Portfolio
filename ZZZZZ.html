INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

The architecture of knowledge is the collection of ideas on how to philosophically handle all knowledge in each context and, in a general way, how to arrange it. The primary aim of this is to provide users with activities and tasks. You need to understand how the pieces fit together to do this and how the objects inside the structure relate to each other. The aim of Information Architecture is to help users know where they are, what they have discovered, and what they can anticipate. (searchstorage, 2005)

Information architecture design typically comes under the care of UX and UI designers or an architect of information. The role of an information architect as a person who bridges users and content by designing search and navigation, embodying abstract ideas into prototypes, units, and disciplines to transform the concepts into something understandable, is explained by Peter Morville, co-author of Information Architecture for the World Wide Web. An information architect who works with a UX designer can focus exclusively on the design of information architecture, while a UX designer spends more time researching. An architect produces a number of deliverables in this case, which we will explain in the section on the steps of IA development. (altexsoft, 2018)
