PROTOTYPING

What is Prototyping?

When it comes to my thoughts of prototyping, the first thing is all about the idea about what you think about the product you want to create. When you are designing your website, it will easily support you and direct you because of the prototype that you do.

In general, prototyping is an important aspect of the nature of Design Thinking and Customer Interface since it helps one to easily validate our ideas and refine them in an equally timely way. A "bias against intervention" is promoted by the College of Architecture at Stanford, where development and research are favored over thought and meeting. (Siang, n.d.)

A prototype is a basic functional model of a potential system used to easily and easily evaluate or verify concepts, design principles and other facets of its conceptualization, so that the designer / s involved may make reasonable refinements or future shifts of direction. (Siang, n.d.)

Prototypes can take different forms, and the only thing the different forms share is that all of them are physical forms of your thoughts. They do not have to be either, far from it, primitive iterations of a product. Examples of concepts are basic drawings or storyboards used to demonstrate a suggested experience solution, rough paper implementations of digital implementations, and even role-playing to act out a service providing a concept. (Siang, n.d.)

Prototyping is about bringing conceptual or theoretical ideas to life before actually developing them and testing their real-world effect. Far too frequently, without adequate analysis or confirmation, concept teams arrive at proposals and expedite them to final implementation before there is any clarity about their feasibility or potential effects on the target market. (Siang, n.d.)
